The Basics of Circuits

(back to home)

A circuit is any path through which electrons can flow.
Circuits are very useful for a multitude of purposes.
These are the basic parts of a circuit:

  • A power source: This provides electrons to power the circuit.

  • A wire: This allows the electrons from the power source to flow, creating a current.

  • A resistor: This resists the flow of electrons. Anything which the circuit provides power to (such as a lightbulb) is a resistor.

Parallel circuits are the other, more complex type of circuit. They differ in having multiple branches, and thus they are still able to function when a resistor goes out.

SAFETY

  • Handle the ends of an uninsulated wire with care. Use erasers and indirectly touch the ends.

  • The wire can get very hot.

  • Just use common sense in general.
