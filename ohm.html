Also, here's some more quick info on each variable of the equation:

  • Voltage

    • Defined as the electric potential energy of the circuit.

    • Measured in volts (unit symbol is V).

  • Current

    • Defined as the flow of electrons through a circuit.

    • Measured in amps/amperes (unit symbol is A).

  • Resistance

    • Defined as the opposition to electrical current in a circuit.

    • Measured in Ohms (unit symbol is Ω).

Stated in words, Ohm's Law looks like this:
Voltage is equal to current multiplied by resistance.
It can also be said like this:
Voltage is directly proportional to current and resistance.

The equation that represents Ohm's Law can be written more than one way, as well:

I=V/R

and as:

R=V/I

Rewriting the equation like this will be very useful for all sorts of problems pertaining to circuits.
