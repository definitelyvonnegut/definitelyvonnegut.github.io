Electricity and magnetism are related forces.
Wires that carry a current create magnetic fields.
This magnetic field can be strengthened by coiling a wire into a solenoid.
It can be further strengthened by inserting a metal core into the solenoid, such as a nail.
Increasing the current of the wire will also strengthen the magnetic field accordingly.
This structure is known as an electromagnet.
It is a powerful tool because it behaves like a magnet when electrified, but otherwise, it has no magnetic properties.
This lab will help you learn all about electromagnetism and how it works.

  • 1 D-size battery

  • Non-galvanized iron nail(s)
    (use more than one if multiple nails are necessary for your experiment; see below)

  • 2 small erasers or other insulative material
    (to contain heat from the ends of the wire)

  • Box of metal paper clips (these will be used in your experiment to test the level of magnetism of your electromagnet.)

The Lab

You already know that increasing the current of the electromagnet (such as by adding a battery of higher voltage) will increase its strength.
But what else will make an electromagnet stronger?
This is what you will be investigating in this lab.

Instructions

  1. Pick one of these factors to test in your experiment:
    Number of wire coils
    Length of nail (use multiple nails of varying lengths; be sure to record their lengths!)
    Length of wire (use multiple lengths of wire; the minimum length should be 5 inches!)
    You will examine how your chosen factor effects paper clip pickups.

  2. Formulate an experiment, including your variables (dependent and independent) and constants, how you will gather data, and how you will analyze the data.
    You will need to include specific details about your experiment.
    You will be attempting to answer the question of if increasing your chosen independent variable (see step 1) causes more paper clips to be picked up.
    Then, get approval on this experiment from your teacher.

  3. Conduct your experiment, gather your data, and analyze it as you planned in the previous step.

  4. Finally, create a poster to report your findings in the format shown below:

    Guiding Question: Will increasing [your factor here] help to pick up more paper clips?
    Claim: [your answer to the guiding question here]
    Evidence Justification
    [put your graphs, analysis, etc. in this column, and explain them + what they mean.] [in this column, explain why your evidence supports the claim you have made using your scientific knowledge and reasoning.]
