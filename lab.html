Electricity and magnetism are related forces.
Wires that carry a current create magnetic fields.
This magnetic field can be strengthened by coiling a wire into a solenoid.
It can be further strengthened by inserting a metal core into the solenoid, such as a nail.
Increasing the current of the wire will also strengthen the magnetic field accordingly.
This structure is known as an electromagnet.
It is a powerful tool because it behaves like a magnet when electrified, but otherwise, it has no magnetic properties.
This lab will help you learn all about electromagnetism and how it works.
